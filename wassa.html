WASSA

Developed a culturally rooted brand identity and packaging system for Wassa,
a Malian spice producer entering the retail market.

THE PROBLEM

Siera B is a young spice producer from Mali with a strong ambition: to enter the competitive retail market. She already had the foundation of her project an idea and a name: Wassa, which means “brave” in her culture. However, like many emerging entrepreneurs, she faced a major challenge: how to stand out among numerous spice producers while remaining authentic to her roots. She wanted more than just a product on a shelf. Her vision was to build a brand that felt vibrant, distinctive, and deeply connected to local production. At this stage, Wassa existed only as a name and an intention. There was no visual identity, no recognizable symbol, and no packaging strategy. The core challenge was to transform this idea into a clear and cohesive brand one that could visually communicate courage, color, culture, and quality.
The mission was therefore twofold:
• To create a strong, memorable visual identity.
• To design packaging that would reflect both the product’s authenticity and its local origins while appealing to a modern market.

THE SOLUTION

The creative process began with exploration. Together, Siera and I worked through multiple sketches and visual directions to define a form that could embody the essence of Wassa. We discussed symbolism, shapes, colors, and cultural references to ensure the identity would feel meaningful and aligned with her story. After two weeks of collaborative and constructive exchanges, we selected and refined a logo that reflected both her personal vision and the brand’s core values. The final mark became the visual anchor of Wassa bold, expressive, and rooted in cultural pride. With the logo established, the next step was to build a coherent brand universe around it. We developed the packaging strategy with a focus on color, clarity, and shelf impact. Particular attention was given to highlighting the authenticity of the spices and emphasizing the importance of local production. Beyond aesthetics, we also shaped a marketing direction that positioned Wassa as more than just spices but as a celebration of bravery, culture, and craftsmanship.
